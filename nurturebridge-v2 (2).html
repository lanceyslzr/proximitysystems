The Risk

AI without
governance
creates risk.

01

Healthcare innovation is accelerating. The gap between deployment and governance is widening.

Many organizations introduce AI before workflows, compliance structures, and oversight mechanisms are fully aligned. That gap creates compounding risk — for patients, for staff, and for the organization. Sabina closes that gap.

"Organizations that approach AI strategically build sustainable efficiency, stronger compliance, improved financial performance, and resilient care delivery systems."

02

AI implemented before workflows are aligned

Automation of broken processes just automates the problems. Workflow redesign must come first.

03

Compliance structures not built for AI

Regulatory standards written for human processes don't automatically apply to AI systems. Specific safeguards are required.

04

Clinical judgment replaced, not enhanced

AI that overrides rather than supports clinicians creates dependency, erodes skill, and ultimately puts patients at risk.

05

No roadmap for ongoing oversight

AI is not a one-time implementation. Without audit mechanisms, risk compounds silently over time.

About Sabina

Frontline nurse.
Frontline leader.

Sabina Ben Kasim is a Family Nurse Practitioner, MSN, RN, and AANP member with over 21 years of experience across frontline nursing, case management, utilization review, leadership, and quality improvement.

As Founder of Nurture Bridge AI Consults, Sabina works alongside healthcare executives, boards, and clinical operations leaders to implement AI responsibly — with governance structures that protect patients, support staff, and build long-term organizational resilience.

Her advisory philosophy is clear: innovation must be governed, efficiency must protect dignity, and technology must enhance — not replace — clinical judgment.
