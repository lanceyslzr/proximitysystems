The DAWN Method

Dementia care
built on dignity.

The DAWN Method is the most research-based, person-centred approach to dementia care available — and Jill is one of the few practitioners in Colorado who is both a certified DAWN Specialist and a certified DAWN Trainer.

DAWN care recognises that people living with dementia are still full human beings with preferences, emotions, and a deep need for connection. It changes how caregivers engage — and how families understand what their loved ones are experiencing.

"We find tremendous meaning in helping families manage care for their loved ones experiencing dementia and make good decisions with their loved one's quality of life as the central focus."
1

See the whole person

DAWN starts with who someone is — their history, preferences, values, and personality — not just their diagnosis or symptoms.

2

Understand the experience

Supporting families to genuinely understand what dementia feels like from the inside — changing how they communicate, respond, and connect.

3

Engage with meaning

Creating moments of real connection, comfort, and purpose — because quality of life isn't lost with a diagnosis, it just needs to be approached differently.

4

Support the whole family

Jill works with everyone involved — not just the person with dementia — because caring for a loved one is a family experience that deserves expert guidance.

About Jill

An OT who chose
to go deeper.

Jill Couch MS OT/L has spent over 30 years as a licensed Occupational Therapist dedicated to older adults — working in senior living communities, home health, and building Better People Care LLC from the ground up in 2018.

She is a certified DAWN Dementia Care Specialist and Trainer, a Beyond Driving with Dignity Certified Professional, and the founder of Pro31 Safe Senior Driver LLC — bringing a rare combination of clinical expertise and genuine compassion to everything she does.

Jill graduated Cum Laude from Texas Woman's University, earned her MS from Colorado State University, completed Army ROTC as a Distinguished Military Graduate, and has taught OT at CSU and the University of North Dakota.
