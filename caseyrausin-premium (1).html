What Casey Sees

When care lacks
structure,
everything suffers.

After over a decade as a COO inside home care and care management organizations, Casey identified the same patterns breaking agencies again and again.



Let's Fix It Together
01

Care becomes reactive instead of proactive

Without structure, teams spend all their time putting out fires. The work gets done but nobody is ever ahead of it — and quality slips quietly over time.

02

Boundaries blur and teams burn out

When roles are unclear and systems don't exist, people absorb everything. The most committed caregivers burn out first — because they care the most.

03

Families lose clarity and confidence

Disorganized operations create inconsistent communication. Families feel unsupported and uninformed — and that erodes the trust your agency depends on.

04

Quality becomes inconsistent

Without documentation standards and clinical frameworks, care varies by caregiver. That's a liability problem, a quality problem, and a growth ceiling — all at once.

05

Growth stalls without sustainable operations

You can't scale what isn't systematized. The most driven agency owners hit a ceiling because the foundation wasn't built to hold more weight.

About Casey

She's been
the COO.
She knows.

Casey Rausin is a Registered Nurse, Certified Case Manager, and former Chief Operating Officer who spent over a decade building and running home care and care management organizations from the inside.

As COO at Choices in Senior Care and Care Around the Block, Casey was responsible for day-to-day clinical care, team performance, service delivery, and operational growth. She saw firsthand what breaks agencies — and built her consulting practice to fix it.

Today through 3C Strategies and the HOMECAREceo Forum, Casey partners with agency owners across the country to build systems that support both people and performance — because she believes you should never have to choose between them.
