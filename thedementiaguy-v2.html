Mark's Unique Framework

The Solar
Approach.

Mark's Solar Approach reframes what care teams call "challenging behaviours" — recognising them instead as meaningful responses to unmet needs or environmental triggers.

When caregivers understand why someone is responding a certain way, they can ask "what can I do to help?" rather than simply reacting. That shift in thinking changes everything about how care is delivered.

"Do we have challenging behaviours when we become upset or angry? Or is it a response to a situation we find ourselves in?"
1

Recognise the change

Understand what is happening in the brain of the person in your care — and why it changes how they experience the world around them.

2

Understand the response

What looks like challenging behaviour is almost always a response to something — an unmet need, a miscommunication, or an environment that doesn't feel safe.

3

Adapt with empathy

Equip your team with a practical toolkit to approach every situation with insight, patience, and a person-first mindset.

4

Deliver with confidence

Your team leaves feeling genuinely capable — not just compliant — of delivering the quality, compassionate care that those in their care deserve.

About Mark

25 years.
One mission.

Mark Griffin MSc is a Dementia Care Specialist and CPD-accredited training provider with over 25 years of dedicated experience supporting individuals living with dementia and the teams that care for them.

His training is rooted in real-world scenarios that care staff encounter daily. Before delivering any session, Mark engages with your management team to understand the specific challenges within your service — ensuring every session is relevant and immediately applicable.

He holds a Master's degree in Dementia Studies from Hull University and is actively pursuing a PhD. His Solar Approach has helped care teams across the UK shift from reactive to genuinely compassionate practice.
