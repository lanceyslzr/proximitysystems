The Risk

What Happens When
AI Goes Ungoverned

Most healthcare AI failures are not technical failures. They are governance failures. Sabina built her practice around the five most common — and most costly — patterns she sees in the field.

01
AI implemented before workflows are ready
Introducing AI into broken or unmapped workflows amplifies inefficiency rather than resolving it. Redesign must come first.
02
No accountability structure for AI decisions
When AI makes a clinical recommendation and something goes wrong, someone must be accountable. Without governance, that structure simply does not exist.
03
Compliance gaps created by AI documentation
AI-generated documentation introduces new risks around accuracy, audit trails, and regulatory standards that traditional compliance frameworks were never designed to address.
04
Clinical judgment replaced, not enhanced
AI that overrides rather than supports clinicians creates dependency, erodes skill, and ultimately puts patients at risk. Enhancement — not replacement — is the only safe path.
05
No roadmap for ongoing oversight
AI is not a one-time implementation. Without audit mechanisms and continuous oversight, risk compounds silently over time until it surfaces in the worst possible moment.
06
Leadership without AI literacy
Boards and executive teams that cannot evaluate AI decisions cannot govern them. Strategic alignment starts with leadership education — not just technical deployment.
